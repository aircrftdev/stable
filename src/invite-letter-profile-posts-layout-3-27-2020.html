Hi I'm Chai Forest with aircrft. Aircrft is the startup network. A place for startups to share what their working on at the moment, get support and help other startups. You could say Aircrft is LinkedIn for startups. We know that sounds impossible to complete with LinkedIn, but we think we can get a toehold initially by inviting 150 mission driven Y Combinator Startup School founders from the 2019/2020 batch to share what their working on, the problem their startup is solving and who the solution is for. Founders can also share unique insights and knowledge their learning along the journey from lift-off to escape velocity. As Sam Alma notes, one of the things that makes Silicon Valley so special is A culture of paying it forward A relentless belief in the future There are people who take wild ideas seriously, instead of mocking you. We've baked these values into the core DNA of the of Aircrft. We believe when we work together, when we join forces, support each other - all ships rise - we all benefit we call this the Aircrft Uplift Algorithm . One of the reasons I'm so passionate about startups is, I've seen first hand the positive impact an founder with an idea on a napkin - who is fueled with passion and perseverance - who is gritty enough to show up everyday and do the deep work, to ship and deliver their first MVP, to make something people want, to make something beautiful and world changing, to ship something that has the power to change lives, create a reverberating wave of abundance and economic impact, and undeniably improve the quality of our lives on planet earth and beyond. As Seth Godin says: "The scarce part is the shipping" - Aircrft is a network of likeminded, purpose driven founders and startup teams that uplift and cheers each other on along this epic journey! You have brilliance in you, your contribution is valuable, only you can do it and you must. Aircrft is your supportive space for diverse and passionate voices - we are honored and humbly invite you to have a seat at the table! We look forward to being inspired by what you make and be a place for sharing your success story. To escape velocity and beyond! - Chai Forest, founder aircrft.com The startup network "Startups helping startups"

I've spent a lot of time thinking about our design process and trying to figure out a better order for us to tackle things. Right now it feels like we're everywhere with tools and process, so here's my suggestion:

  1. Define the goals: Create a template for expressing what the purpose of a project is and why we're investing time and money in tackling it.
  2. Sketch a solution: Use tried and true paper and pencil to express ideas and share them with others at the company before going too deep on design.
  3. User test with Figma: Use the page linking in Figma to get a rough clickable prototype and test this with real users.
  4. Prototype with code: Built and HTML/CSS with dummied data to test how things feel before building a true front-end.
